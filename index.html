🔥 What has Trump been up to in the past 24 hours?

Deploying the Tariff "Nuclear Option"
Donald Trump announced a sweeping tariff and reciprocal tax plan in the White House Rose Garden, proposing a baseline 10% levy on all goods imported to the U.S. and higher tariffs targeting dozens of nations, triggering massive market turmoil. While Treasury Secretary urged countries to avoid retaliation, multiple nations have condemned the move and vowed countermeasures.
Beer and Empty Aluminum Cans Added to Tariff List
The Trump administration declared a 25% tariff on all imported beer, while also categorizing beer and empty aluminum cans as derivative products subject to existing aluminum tariffs.
Claims About Musk's Imminent Departure
Sources revealed Trump told cabinet officials that Elon Musk would "soon leave the White House to refocus on his companies," though he might retain an informal advisory role. Musk later dismissed the reports as "false."
Securing Pro Bono Legal Deals
Trump announced an agreement with Milbank LLP, requiring the firm to provide at least $100 million in pro bono legal services during his potential second term. Three law firms have reportedly joined the arrangement.
Plans for an "External Revenue Service"
According to U.S. media, Trump is poised to unveil plans this Wednesday to establish an External Revenue Service (ERS), a cornerstone of his new trade agenda aimed at collecting tariff revenues globally.
Approval Ratings Hit Record Low
A Reuters/Ipsos poll shows Trump's approval rating has plunged to 43%, the lowest since his return to office, driven by public dissatisfaction with his tariff policies and the administration's handling of Yemen strike disclosures.
Inviting El Salvador's President to the White House
Salvadoran President Nayib Bukele confirmed receiving a private invitation from Trump for an April 14 visit. Trump's letter praised El Salvador's cooperation in curbing illegal immigration, crediting the nation for "historic progress in securing America's borders."
